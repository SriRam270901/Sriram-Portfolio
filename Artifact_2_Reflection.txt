Artifact 2 Reflection

For my second artifact, I selected SystemBridge AI because it demonstrates a different competency from my first artifact. The leadership roadmap focused on self-assessment, professional development, and change leadership, while SystemBridge AI provides tangible evidence of applied generative AI design and development. I customized the portfolio presentation for recruiters, instructors, and technical professionals by reducing course-specific language and emphasizing the user problem, design decisions, tools, responsible-AI boundaries, and business value.

The most important lesson I learned was that building a useful AI application involves more than connecting a model to a web interface. The quality of the result depends on defining a narrow purpose, designing prompts and behavioral constraints, testing difficult scenarios, protecting sensitive information, and clearly communicating uncertainty. Feedback and evaluation during the AI Lab encouraged me to make the assistant less likely to jump to conclusions. I applied that learning by requiring it to separate known facts from assumptions, ask focused questions, prioritize low-risk checks, and avoid claiming access to systems or evidence it does not possess.

I also revised the portfolio itself so visitors can quickly understand what the artifact is, why it was created, how it was developed, which technologies were used, and what professional value it demonstrates. The live product and supporting documentation provide direct evidence of the work. Together, the two artifacts now show both leadership development and hands-on AI application skills, giving a broader and more accurate picture of my growth in AI and machine learning.
